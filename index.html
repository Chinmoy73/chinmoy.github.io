Dr. Chinmoy Dev Nath is a dedicated public health researcher with specialized expertise in microbiology, genomics, epidemiology, data sciences, and the One Health approach. He holds a Doctor of Veterinary Medicine (DVM) degree (2019–2025) from Bangladesh Agricultural University and is currently pursuing a Master’s in Public Health under the mentorship of Professor Dr. Mahbubul Pratik Siddique in the Department of Microbiology & Hygiene. Since March 2025, he has been contributing as a graduate research assistant at the MPS Molecular Biology and Nanotechnology Lab, where he is engaged in advanced molecular and genomic research related to public health.

His research interests span a wide range of interdisciplinary areas, including bacterial pathogenesis, molecular and cellular microbiology, zoonotic disease transmission, food- and waterborne infections, and metagenomic analysis. He applies next-generation sequencing, bioinformatics tools, and epidemiological methods to explore microbial threats across human, animal, and environmental interfaces. His work is grounded in the One Health framework, which emphasizes integrated strategies to improve health outcomes in diverse populations.

Dr. Nath is committed to multidisciplinary collaboration and data-driven research to address pressing health challenges in both local and global contexts. He is actively seeking higher studies and international research opportunities to expand his skills and contribute to impactful scientific advancements in infectious disease epidemiology, genomic surveillance, and public health innovation.
